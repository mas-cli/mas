# Code of Conduct

Participants must interact with integrity & professionalism.

## Prohibited Conduct

Prohibited conduct includes:

- Malicious contributions or advice
- Doxxing
- Threats
- Insults
- Harassment
- Sexualization
- Trolling
- Spamming
- Advertising
- Plagiarism
- Impersonation
- False representation
- Conduct that could reasonably be deemed unacceptable in a professional
  environment

## Reporting

To report violations privately to moderators (instead of to GitHub Support),
click the **…** (three dot) menu on the relevant issue, PR, or comment, then
select **Report content**.

- If a reporting dialog appears, select **To repository admins**, then
  follow the instructions
- If no reporting dialog appears, instead create a [security
  advisory](https://github.com/mas-cli/mas/security/advisories/new), including
  links to, and attachments documenting, the alleged violations

Moderators are obligated to maintain complainant confidentiality & to
investigate complaints.

Complainants will be notified about an investigation's outcome & about any
actions taken.

## Enforcement

Moderators are responsible for maintaining community standards & for taking
corrective action in response to reported prohibited conduct.

Moderators maintain the responsibility & right to:

- **Moderate Content:** Reject, remove, hide, redact, or edit violating content
- **Restrict Access:** Temporarily or permanently ban violating individuals,
  accounts, services, or other entities

Moderators who do not in good faith follow or enforce this Code of Conduct may
face temporary or permanent repercussions, as determined by other moderators.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces & wherever an individual,
account, service, or other entity represents—or purports to represent—the
project or organization.
